Pacific Crest Institute (PCI) — Master Overview

1. Identity & Mission
Pacific Crest Institute (PCI) is a San Diego–based academic services company founded by Ethan Alvarée, Molly Sun, and Kun Xuan as equal partners. PCI provides:
•	Academic tutoring
•	Test preparation
•	Enrichment programs
•	Digital art and creative media courses (in partnership with a professional publisher)
•	Portfolio building opportunities for students
Mission: Deliver high quality, student centered academic and creative instruction that empowers learners to grow, build confidence, and prepare for college and career pathways.

2. Leadership Structure
All three founders are equal business partners.
Kun Xuan — Strategic Advisor & Co Founder
•	Provides strategic guidance, long term planning, and business development
•	Supports high level partnerships and institutional relationships
Ethan Alvarée — CEO
•	Oversees curriculum, instruction, academic quality, and program development
•	Leads technology, systems, and operational infrastructure
•	Manages partnerships related to curriculum, publishing, and academic content
Molly Sun — Director of Marketing, Recruitment, and Administration
•	Oversees admissions, family communication, and operational workflows
•	Manages customer experience, onboarding, and administrative systems
•	Leads community engagement and parent facing communication

3. Governance Principles
PCI is intentionally structured to be:
•	Clean — no blurred lines with HBA or PCHS
•	Separate — its own brand, domain, and operational identity
•	Transparent — no conflicts of interest in partnerships or contracts
PCI does not rely on HBA infrastructure, accounts, or governance. PCI will eventually operate from its own Microsoft 365 tenant.

4. Technology & Infrastructure Plan
Phase 1 — Startup Phase (Current)
•	Operate temporarily within the HBA Microsoft 365 tenant
•	Use domain based PCI accounts (not aliases)
•	Maintain separate PCI folders, Teams, and SharePoint sites
•	Keep PCI data isolated from HBA data
Phase 2 — Growth Phase
•	Register pacificcrestinstitute.com
•	Create PCI specific email accounts (info@, admin@, ethan@, molly@, kun@)
•	Build PCI’s own SharePoint, Teams, and OneDrive structure
•	Establish PCI’s Google Business Profile
Phase 3 — Independence Phase
•	Create a dedicated PCI Microsoft 365 tenant
•	Migrate PCI users, SharePoint sites, Teams, and OneDrives
•	Transfer the PCI domain to the new tenant
•	Maintain clean separation from HBA and PCHS
This ensures PCI can scale without technical debt.

5. Branding & Public Presence
PCI will maintain:
•	Its own website
•	Its own YouTube channel
•	Its own Google Business listing
•	Its own social media presence
•	Its own visual identity and messaging
PCI is not a subsidiary of HBA or PCHS — it is a standalone educational services company.

6. Partnership With FIP / Ken
PCI’s partnership with the publisher must include:
•	Co producer credit on all books where PCI students contribute
•	Student credit structure that clearly defines how students are credited
•	Limited but real creative oversight, including:
o	design elements
o	production quality
o	print proofs
•	Non exclusive promotional rights for PCI to use student work in marketing
•	Clear boundaries preventing PCI from being treated as a freelancer or labor provider
PCI must retain:
•	creative dignity
•	institutional credit
•	student protection
•	brand integrity

7. Conflict of Interest Safeguards
PCI must remain cleanly separated from:
HBA
•	PCI does not make decisions for HBA
•	PCI does not use HBA resources without approval
•	PCI does not influence HBA governance
•	PCI does not create financial entanglements
PCHS (future nonprofit)
•	PCI may partner with PCHS, but:
o	PCHS board must approve any agreements
This keeps PCI compliant with nonprofit governance rules.

8. Long Term Vision
PCI is designed to grow into:
•	A premium academic services provider
•	A creative arts and digital media hub
•	A partner for schools, publishers, and educational organizations
•	A scalable, multi program educational brand
PCI’s structure supports future expansion into:
•	Online courses
•	Digital art academies
•	Curriculum development
•	Publishing partnerships
•	College prep programs
•	Summer institutes

9. Summary
PCI is:
•	A clean, independent academic services company
•	Co founded by Kun, Ethan, and Molly
•	Structured to avoid conflicts with HBA and PCHS
•	Built for long term scalability
•	Supported by a clear governance and technology roadmap
•	Positioned for partnerships, publishing, and creative expansion
