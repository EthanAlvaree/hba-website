The Opportunity: 
Beyond "Studio Art"
• The Current State: Our Friday-only "Studio 
Art" elective class provides a valuable 
creative outlet, but lacks the time, tools, and 
training required for modern digital mastery.
• The Gap:Some students are already meeting 
during lunch and after school, but there is 
not enough time to learn and reach mastery.
• Current Limit: Students only have time for 
"flatting" (simple coloring).
• The Goal:Teach advanced skills like lighting, 
shadows, and storytelling.
• The Need:A structured Mon–Thu schedule 
allows for "deep work" and finished, 
professional-grade portfolios.
Real Skills vs. 
AI Generation
• Previously: Last year, the digital art class 
at HBA focused only on AI generation.
• Human Artistry: We teach students to 
draw and paint by hand.
• No AI Shortcuts: We do not use "AI 
prompts." We build real talent.
• The "How" and "Why": Students learn 
why every line and color matters.
• Core Skills: Learning form, light, 
storytelling, and professional tools.
• The Outcome: Students learn to create
art, not just "ask" a computer to do it.

HBA

1. Drawing and Lineart
Fundamentals
• Anatomy fundamentals
• Shape language and character design
• Silhouette clarity, visual readability, 
avoiding tangent lines
• Environment sketching and spatial 
design
• Technical drawing: perspective
Foundations of Light
• Types of light sources– direct (key/center)– rim lights– ambient light– fill lights
• Lighting zone phenomena– Specular highlights, – center (diffuse) light, – form light and halftones, – Terminator– reflected / bounce light
• Light Behavior and Physics– Emission strength, distance, and fall off (inverse square law)– Light transmission (subsurface scattering, reflections, and 
refractions)
• Material response (metal, glass, fabric, skin, fur, etc.)
Foundations of 
Shadow
• Types of Shadows– Form shadow– Core shadow– Cast shadow– Occlusion/crevice shadow
• Shadow Zone Phenomena– Terminator– Halftones– Reflected / bounce light– Umbra and penumbra
• Shadow Behavior & Physics– Shadow softness (light size and distance)– Falloff inside shadows– Ambient light influence
• Material Response (matte vs. glossy, rough vs. 
smooth, transparent vs. opaque, etc.)
Color Theory
• 3 Dimensions of Color– Value, – Saturation, – Hue
• Color Relationships– Complementary colors– Analogous colors– Triadic and split-complementary schemes– Warm vs. cool palettes
• Color Changes with Light Angle– Surfaces facing light are brighter– Turning away from light makes colors darker– Halftones become more saturated– Hue shifts depending on the mix of light color 
and local color– Shadows show the color of reflected light
Color Theory in 
Practice
• Color behavior– Color temperature– Color & Context: simultaneous 
contrast and Chevreul’s Laws– Atmospheric perspective– Local color vs. lighting color
• Visual focus & Storytelling– Using Value contrast to guide the 
eye– Using saturation to emphasize 
focal points– Color hierarchy and readability– Mood and emotional tone
Storytelling 
with Color
• Visual Narrative: Using color to tell 
the story without words
• Atmospheric Control:Mastering 
palette shifts to dictate the 
"temperature" of a scene: e.g. “hot”, 
“cold”, or “scary”.
• Emotional Keying:Using specific 
palettes to show how a character 
feels.
• Compositional Focus:Using color 
contrast to guide the viewer’s eye.
• Smart Choices:Moving from "just 
coloring" to choosing colors with a 
plan.

Software and 
Digital Production
• Industry-Standard Formats: Mastery of 
layered .PSD and Clip Studio Paint pipelines.
• Asset Management:Professional file 
organization and naming conventions.
• Non-Destructive Workflows: Utilizing masks 
and adjustment layers for revisions, so original 
art is never lost or destroyed.
• Selection Tools: Mastering "Magic Wand" and 
freehand tools for speed.
• Custom Toolsets:Creating and changing 
brushes to fit different materials and art styles.
Software and Layers
• Types of Layers: Background, flats, 
rendering, inks, holds, lettering
• Layer modes: normal, multiply, 
overlay, hardlight, etc.
• Adjustment layers and blending 
modes
• Selection and masking techniques 
(clipping masks, layer masks)
• Transformations: gaussian blur, 
binarization, converting brightness to 
opacity, correcting line weight, 
removing dust, auto-actions
The Process: Inks, Flats, Holds, and Rendering
Final layer: Lettering

Industry Exposure: Guest Feedback 
Sessions with Professionals
Ken Penders is a longtime author, 
illustrator, and publisher best known for his 
work on Sonic the Hedgehog from 1993 to 
2006. He created many original characters 
and storylines, including writing every issue 
of Knuckles the Echidna (1997) and the 
Princess Sally (1995) miniseries. 
Beyond Sonic, Ken has worked on titles such 
as Star Trek, King of the Hill, and The Legend 
of Zelda. He has also worked in animation, 
story development, filmmaking, and 
independent publishing. His studio, Floating 
Island Productions, is currently releasing 
hardcover editions of his original stories.
Scott Shaw is an award-winning penciller, 
animator, and writer with over 50 years of 
experience. He was the first artist on Archie 
Comics’ Sonic the Hedgehog series (1992), 
recently collected in Floating Island Productions’ 
hardcover Scott Shaw’s Sonic Days. He also 
worked as co-creator on The Simpsons comic 
books and classic properties like The Flintstones. 
Scott has taught cartooning and animation at the 
college level and co-founded San Diego 
Comic-Con in 1970. His expertise in visual humor, 
character design, and production pipelines offers 
students an inspiring look into the professional art 
world.
Kurt Michael Russell has been working as a 
professional digital colorist since 2011, 
known for his work on comics published by 
DC, Image, Vault, IDW, Dark Horse, and 
other major publishers. 
Since 2014, Kurt also teaches digital 
coloring through popular online courses 
and YouTube, helping thousands of artists 
learn digital coloring. His expertise in 
lighting, mood, rendering, and digital 
workflow gives students practical, 
industry-ready skills.
