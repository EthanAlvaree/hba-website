1. The Core Strategic Insight
PCI should not try to be just another test prep center.
The test prep market is saturated, price competitive, and dominated by incumbents (AoPS, IvyMax, etc.) which makes competing in this space difficult.
PCI should become a hybrid academic + creative institute, offering a portfolio-building program with real industry output via a unique publishing pipeline.
This is a category of one. No competitor in San Diego—or nationally—can offer:
•	Digital art instruction
•	Professional graphic novel production workflow
•	Guaranteed publication credit
•	Industry mentorship
•	Portfolio development
•	College application ready creative résumé
•	A real publisher partnership
This is not “another tutoring center.” This is a creative tech learning institute with a built in professional pipeline. This becomes PCI’s differentiator:
“We don’t just teach — we publish.”
2. The Art Institute Concept (PCI × FIP Partnership)

What PCI Should Negotiate For From FIP
1.	Co Producer Credit for PCI
This costs FIP Publishing nothing and is essential.
Why: PCI is providing:
•	labor (students, assistants, us)
•	facilities
•	instruction
•	workflow management
•	production oversight
•	quality control
That is producer work.
Ask: PCI is credited as Co Producer on all books where PCI students contribute.
PCI should ask for:
•	“Produced in partnership with Pacific Crest Institute” inside books
•	PCI logo in credits page
This builds PCI’s brand massively.

2.	A Formalized Student Credit Structure
PCI should define:
•	How students are credited
•	Where their names appear
•	What qualifies as “contribution”
•	How PCI is credited
This protects students and PCI.

3.	Creative Oversight Rights (Limited but Real)
We’re not asking to override his storytelling. We’re asking for shared approval rights:
•	Approval on design layouts and elements (TOC, foreword/back matter layout, typography, color consistency, print proofs before going to press)
•	Approval on production quality (no typos, no layout errors)
•	Approval on print proofs before going to press
•	Participation in book selection roadmap
•	Participation in release schedule planning
This is not about ego or taking control. This is simply accountability, quality control, and protecting the brand — his and ours. This is standard in co production.

4.	 A Revised Compensation Structure
Right now Ethan is being paid:
•	$200 per hardcover
•	For months of work
•	While other creatives are being paid twice (once in the 90s/00s, once now)
•	And Ethan is being paid once
This is not sustainable.
What PCI should ask for:
Percentage of net revenue
PCI receives 5–10% of net revenue from each book it co produces.

5.	 Rights to Use the Work for PCI Marketing
PCI needs the right to:
•	Show student work in portfolios
•	Show process videos
•	Advertise the program
•	Use images in brochures, website, social media
Ask: PCI receives non exclusive promotional rights to use any PCI produced pages for marketing.
This is standard and harmless to FIP Publishing.

6.	Long-term rights / exclusivity (VERY strategic)
PCI should aim for first-look / right-of-first-refusal.
Meaning:
If FIP Publishing wants to:
•	Reprint books
•	Release new collections
•	Remaster old stories
PCI gets first opportunity to produce them.
This protects our past work and future work.
We’re not buying FIP. We’re building a pathway toward deeper partnership.
Later, this could evolve into:
•	Distribution partnership 
•	Partial acquisition 
•	Licensing deal 
But don’t jump straight to acquisition. Start with exclusivity & first rights.

A Long Term Option for PCI to Acquire FIP
We don’t ask to buy FIP now. We ask for an option.
Ask: PCI receives a Right of First Negotiation and Right of First Refusal if Ken Penders ever sells FIP or its publishing rights.
This is legally standard and not threatening.
It ensures:
•	We protect our work
•	PCI can continue the legacy
•	Kun can invest strategically
•	No one else swoops in and takes the rights
This is the smartest long term move we can make.
